Answer: To get a Social Security Number (SSN), you need to follow these steps based on the provided documents:

1. **Complete the Online Application**: Start by completing the Online Social Security Number Application. After completing the application, you will receive a reference number that begins with the letter "O". Write down this reference number.

2. **Prepare Required Documents**: You'll need to bring the following documents to the Social Security Office:
   - A printed and signed I-20 form.
   - A printed copy of your I-94 Arrival/Departure Record from the CBP I-94 website.
   - Your passport.
   - An employment letter signed by your job supervisor and an ISE staff member.
   - The application reference number from your online application.

3. **Schedule an Appointment**: You should be prompted to schedule an appointment at the conclusion of your application. If you are not prompted or if you're under 18 and cannot complete the online application, call the Concord SSA Office to request an appointment. Have your application reference number handy.

4. **Application Tips**: 
   - Make sure your name matches across all documents (passport, visa, I-94, and I-20) without using nicknames.
   - Indicate that you are a "legal alien allowed to work".
   - Include your parents' full names but leave their SSNs blank unless they have a U.S. Social Security Number.
   - Use your mother's maiden name for her name.
   - Use your Davidson College mailing address as this is where your card will be sent.

5. **After Receiving Your SSN**: Once you receive your SSN in the mail, visit the Human Resources office on campus to share your SSN. Also, update your SSN in Sprintax Calculus. Keep your Social Security card in a safe place and do not share it via email. If you have an Individual Tax Identification Number (ITIN), notify the IRS about your new SSN to merge your accounts and close your ITIN.

These steps will guide you through obtaining a Social Security Number.

  Doc 3: Taxes
Every person in the United States who earns money (from a job, from a scholarship, etc.) must pay income tax. This tax is usually deducted from individual's paychecks throughout the year. At the end of every tax year, employers and Davidson College send out summaries of how much money employees/students earned or were given (scholarships, grants, etc.), and how much tax was taken out of that money.
